5 ‚Äî Non-fiction Is the Hardest Genre to Sell

Unless you hit the right topic at the right time ‚Äî like I would have if I published TPC when the pandemic was still on ‚Äî going for non-fiction is the hardest way you can debut in the editorial industry.

But, if the sole fatigue of writing as if you were at university was not enough, non-fiction is also the hardest genre to sell. I learned this too late. If only I had watched Ryan Holiday advices, now I would know why.

Non-fiction is usually written by experts, discussing their own fields, and their goal is different from what novel authors want to achieve. The latter want to sell their books, as this is their source of income. The former want to use their book as a promotional vehicle to sell their services. See the difference? The expert‚Äôs writing journey is this:

They become good at something, for example financial markets,
They open a small hedge fund specialized in emerging markets,

These people are impressed by the book, thinking ‚ÄúHoly shit! They know their game! I want to get in touch with them and see if they can manage my portfolio!‚Äù
The book sells ‚Äújust‚Äù 1,000 copies, but to the right people who will bring millions under the author‚Äôs hedge fund‚Äôs umbrella!
Numbers of non-fiction sales are around the few thousands of copies. If you dream to publish a best seller, you may want to look at another genre. I know, we all dream to write the next ‚ÄúThe Subtle Art of Not Giving a F*ck‚Äù but those are exceptions, fruit of a mix of viral marketing ability and luck with timing. 99% of the time it won‚Äôt happen to you. The only other way I can think non-fiction can sell is if you are a celebrity and write your own biography. But you need to become famous first‚Ä¶



6 ‚Äî Send Your Manuscript to Writing Contests

Alert: send it BEFORE you publish your book. The reason is that 90% of contests want unpublished works. There are several reasons for that, but the most obvious is that the contest organization wants to be the one who discovered the new gem of the world literature.

Most contests require a very cheap entry, and some are even free. No matter what you wrote or how peculiar might feel to you: there will always be a niche for you. Find it and you will get a lot of promotion for free.

Regardless of the genre you choose, you need to build hype around your publication. This is valid both for fiction and non-fiction.

What should you do in these 6 months? Here are a few ideas:
